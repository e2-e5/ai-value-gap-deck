CEO / Founder Brief · v0.1

The AI value gap
is not a model problem.

Most companies deploy AI faster than they create value. The trap is treating AI as a wrapper or pilot. The winners build AI as infrastructure.

Roland Berger backbone Why wrappers fail AI as infrastructure

The signal

AI adoption is up.
Value realization is not.

Roland Berger’s 2026 report frames the core issue clearly: companies are investing aggressively, but returns lag spending for the overwhelming majority.

~90% of firms report returns lagging spending
99% formal leadership involvement in AI
~40% primarily rely on off-the-shelf AI solutions
~10% consistently capture meaningful impact
Source: Roland Berger, “The AI value gap”, 2026

What is really failing

Most companies don’t have an AI model problem.
They have an operating model problem.

Wrong dashboard

They measure launches, pilots, or tokens — not time-to-value, workflow conversion, margin impact, or decision velocity.

Shallow integration

They buy modern AI but attach it to brittle legacy workflows. The surface looks smart; the system underneath stays dumb.

No compounding loop

Every use case is funded as a one-off project, so knowledge, telemetry, governance, and reusable tooling never compound.

Why AI wrappers fail

Wrappers are great for demos.
Bad at carrying operational load.

Wrapper logic

  • Fast to ship on top of someone else’s model
  • Thin UI over prompts and APIs
  • Limited control over data, quality, governance
  • Hard to defend when distribution or pricing shifts
  • Breaks when workflows, permissions, or edge cases get real
→

Infrastructure logic

  • AI is wired into data, workflows, and system boundaries
  • Shared evaluation, observability, and fallback layers
  • Governance is embedded, not added later
  • New use cases become cheaper over time
  • Advantage compounds through reuse and operational learning

Roland Berger’s answer

The winners are not the fastest adopters.
They are the industrializers.

Industrializers don’t spend more or move faster. They run AI differently: deeper ownership, tighter standards, embedded governance, and ongoing operational improvement.

Own the system

Retain control even when working with partners.

Rewire, don’t wrap

Embed AI into enterprise workflows and data flows.

Govern in-platform

Standards and controls live in the platform, not in PowerPoint committees.

Fund compounding capability

Invest in shared platforms, not isolated pilots.

AI as infrastructure

Think less about “AI features”.
Think more about the AI stack.

Interface layerco-pilots, agents, internal tools, product UX
Workflow layerorchestration, approvals, automations, human-in-the-loop
Intelligence layermodels, prompting, routing, memory, tools, evaluation
Trust layerobservability, policy, security, audit, cost controls
Data layersource systems, context, permissions, retrieval, feedback loops

Implication for CEOs and founders

The mandate is not “launch more AI”.
The mandate is orchestrate value creation.

01

Fund the platform

Budget shared eval, observability, data access, and governance instead of only use-case POCs.

02

Change the KPI

Reward repeatable business impact, not demo velocity or pilot count.

03

Build cross-functional ownership

AI cannot sit in one innovation silo; it must span product, data, operations, finance, and risk.

Practical next step

A 90-day founder agenda

  • Audit current AI initiatives by business value and architectural depth
  • Kill thin wrappers with no path to system-level advantage
  • Pick 1–2 core workflows to rewire end-to-end
  • Stand up shared eval + observability + policy rails
  • Make one exec owner accountable for time-to-value

“You cannot buy AI transformation — you must engineer it.”

Roland Berger framing, adapted for CEO audience

Bottom line

AI wrappers capture attention.
AI infrastructure captures value.
